Frame and Fancy





Watercolor paint is a transparent medium made from pigments mixed with water-soluble binders. It works by allowing light to pass through the thin layers of paint, giving the colors a luminous, glowing effect. When applied to paper, the pigment bonds with the fibers, and the water evaporates, leaving behind vibrant, subtle washes of color. Artists can manipulate the intensity and flow of watercolor by adjusting the amount of water, creating beautiful gradients, textures, and fluid designs. The unique nature of watercolor allows for both delicate, soft tones and bold, striking contrasts, making it a versatile and expressive medium.
